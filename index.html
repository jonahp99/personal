SafeChat

For me, this project was an exploration of cryptography as well as my first foray into full-stack web development. It was the culmination of all the skills I'd learned in school up until that point, and my first attempt at tackling a new topic entirely foreign to me.

At a time when Facebook is reading users' messages and online privacy has never been more important, I wanted to create something that allowed people to communicate online with confidence that no third party is reading their conversations. I implemented RSA encryption to enable just that -- users of my site can encrypt sensitive information and send it over any medium without fear of their message being read by anyone not intended to view it. The site also permits unregistered users a demo with fewer features if they don't want to create an account.

Life in Color

My inspiration for Life in Color came from a journal I came across. This journal had a twist: in the back there was a calendar with 365 contiguous squares. To the right of this was a color-code, with a spectrum of colors and associated emotions. The idea for this journal was that for each day you journaled, you also would pick a predominant emotion for that day and color the calendar accordingly. At the end of the year, you could reflect on the past year and find trends in your emotions.

My site is similar. Every day you make a journal entry that is assigned to a spot on your calendar "Spectrum" page. However, rather than picking an emotion manually, I use sentiment analysis to assign emtions to your daily entries automatically. Building this project was incredibly rewarding, and allowed me combine my technical skills with my creativity to create something totally new.
